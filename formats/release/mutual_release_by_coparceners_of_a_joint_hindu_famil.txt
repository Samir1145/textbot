MUTUAL RELEASE BY COPARCENERS OF A JOINT HINDU FAMIL

THIS DEED of Release made at .......this ………..day	of 20 ………..between A, son of ………..of the ONE PART, ………..B, son of ………..of the SECOND PART, C, son of ………..of the THIRD PART and D, son of of the FOURTH  PART.
 
WHEREAS X, son of 	expired on………..at………..leaving behind certain immovable and movable properties and four sons the said A, B, C and D.
 
AND WHEREAS the said A has gone and is settled in ………..for the last 10 years and no longer willing to take share in the properties of the Joint Hindu family.
 
AND WHEREAS the said B, C and D have 6ftered him some family jewellery and precious items, more particularly described in the Schedule there under written out of the joint Hindu family property and the said A has agreed to relinquish his right, title and interest in the entire joint Hindu family property except the said jewellery and precious items.
 
NOW THIS DEED WITNESSETH AS FOLLOWS: 
(1) In pursuance of the agreement and in consideration of the premises, the said A hereby relinquishes and releases all his rights, title, interest and claim in the said joint Hindu family properties and family business and hereby discharges the other coparceners of their obligation to partition the joint Hindu family properties with him, save and except the jewellery and precious items more particularly described in the Schedule hereto, which shall remain in his exclusive possession, use, occupation and ownership to the exclusion of all the other parties hereto.
 
(2) The said B, C and D hereby declare and confirm that they or any of them, shall have no right, title, interest or claim in the said jewellery and precious items more particularly described in the Schedule hereto and that the said jewellery and precious items shall be solely owned by 	the said A and shall be deemed to have ceased to belong to the said joint Hindu family.
 
(3) Each of the parties hereto covenants with the other or others that he has not done or suffered to be done anything whereby joint family properties or any part thereof may be burdened in any manner and indemnifies the other or others against any claim, demand, proceeding, suit, cost or expenses that may hereafter arise by his act or omission.
